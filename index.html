Rooting for Equity

A Transformative 3-Year Business Plan

Executive Summary

Rooting for Equity is a transformative initiative combining sustainable agriculture and economic empowerment for Black, Indigenous, and underserved communities. It teaches hydroponic gardening, sustainable practices, and creative monetization strategies to foster community resilience and economic independence.

Mission Statement

To empower underserved communities through sustainable agriculture, cooperative practices, and economic justice, fostering food sovereignty and wealth-building.

Goals and Objectives

Year 1: Pilot and Foundation Building

  • Serve 8 participants with hydroponic kits and workshops.
  • Educate participants on monetizing harvests (e.g., teas, soaps).
  • Secure $50,000 in funding.

Year 2: Growth and Expansion

  • Expand program to 20 participants.
  • Plan a centralized workspace or warehouse.

Year 3: Regional Impact and Sustainability

  • Establish a hemp and rice production facility.
  • Develop a CSA subscription model for 50+ households.

Key Activities

  • Develop advanced hydroponics and cooperative management workshops.
  • Build partnerships with local markets.
  • Launch hempcrete tiny homes for sustainable housing.

Revenue Model

Short-Term

  • Workshop fees and sales of participant goods (teas, soaps).

Mid-Term

  • CSA subscriptions and hempcrete product sales.

Long-Term

  • Cooperative profits from cash crops (hemp, rice).
  • Rental/sale of hempcrete tiny homes.
